Mandy Rose Was Making More Money Off Premium Content Than From Her WWE Contract

Publish date: 2024-08-16

Mandy Rose dropped the NXT Women’s Title in surprising fashion this week, because that match against Roxanne Perez wasn’t supposed to go down until New Year’s Evil. Then Mandy Rose was released the next day due to the content she was putting up on her premium content service behind paywall. It turns out that she had a big reason to keep up that hustle.

During Wrestling Observer Live, Bryan Alvarez noted that Mandy Rose was making “more money” from her paywall content than she was making under her WWE contract. It wasn’t noted exactly what ballpark of money we’re talking about here, but Mandy Rose was under a WWE main roster deal as NXT Women’s Champion.

Fans who want to view Mandy Rose’s premium content must subscribe to her monthly service for $30. Then they will have an opportunity to purchase content ala carte, and it isn’t cheap. We’ve seen Mandy Rose fans drop $200 on one photo in the past. Needless to say, that money adds up.

Mandy Rose knew that WWE didn’t want her posting this kind of content. It was quite evident by how she tried to squash the story from gaining momentum. It is unclear how many chances WWE gave her, but it resulted in her termination in the end.

We’ll have to see what’s next for Mandy Rose, but she will likely keep up her premum content hustle, because she doesn’t have a morality clause to worry about now.
