&quot;I Won&quot;: Nigerian Chess Master Plays Against 10 Oyinbo People At Same Time, Defeats All of Them

Publish date: 2024-08-08
  • Chess master, Tunde Onakoya, was in Germany recently, and he took part in a fierce competition
  • He played against 10 people, simultaneously moving around and engaging them in a hot chess game
  • Tunde, who runs Chess in Slums initiative, revealed on X that at the end of the game, he emerged as the winner

A chess master from Nigeria, Tunde Onakoya, has shown how good he is at the game.

On Day 2 of the DLD (Digital – Life – Design) conference in Germany, he simultaneously engaged 10 players at the same time.

Tunde of Chess in Slums posted a video of the fierce competition on X.

In the video, Tunde was spotted moving around and engaging the 10 different players who were up against him.

He revealed that he was able to defeat all of them and came out as the champion after almost two hours.
