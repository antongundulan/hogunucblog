How Long is Long Beach, Washington? You Might Need a REALLY Big Towel

Ever dreamed of a beach stretching as far as the eye can see? A place where your biggest worry is getting sand between your toes (and other less pleasant places)? Well, my friend, pack your swimsuit and sunscreen, because Long Beach, Washington, might just be your paradise.

It's Long, Like, REALLY Long... But How Long Exactly?

Alright, alright, so we're laying it on a bit thick here. But seriously, Long Beach isn't messing around when it comes to beachfront property. This beauty boasts a whopping 28 miles (45 kilometers) of continuous sandy goodness. That's right, 28 miles! You could walk for days (or rent a cool electric scooter, we won't judge) and still have more beach to explore.

Fun Fact: Long Beach likes to claim the title of "Longest Stretch of Driveable Beach in the World." Now, while there might be some debate about that (looking at you, Daytona Beach), there's no denying it's one heck of a long walk.

Hold on, Can You Drive on the Beach?

Yep, you read that right! Unlike most beaches where you're stuck building sandcastles next to a giant parking lot, Long Beach lets you get your inner beach bum on with a designated driving area. Just be sure to check the tide tables and local regulations before you hit the gas. Nobody wants to get stuck in the sand, literally.

Pro Tip: Sunsets over the Pacific Ocean from your car? Now that's what we call a beachfront drive-in movie!

So, What Can You Do on a Beach That Long?

Let's just say you won't be complaining about having nothing to do. Here are just a few ideas:

  • Fly a Kite: With all that open space, your kite will be thanking you.
  • Build Sandcastle Palaces: Because why settle for a measly sandcastle when you have an entire beach kingdom to create?
  • Beachcombing Bonanza: Keep your eyes peeled for seashells, cool rocks, and maybe even some hidden pirate treasure (okay, maybe not the treasure, but hey, a man can dream!)
  • Horseback Riding: Yep, you can gallop along the shore on a majestic steed. Just be sure to check with local stables for rentals and regulations.
  • Nature Walks and Wildlife Watching: Long Beach has plenty of natural beauty to explore, from tide pools teeming with life to spotting majestic bald eagles soaring overhead.

Basically, the only limit is your imagination (and maybe your sunscreen application schedule).

How To FAQs: Long Beach Beachin' Basics

How to Get There: Long Beach is a popular getaway, so you have options! You can drive in, fly into nearby airports and rent a car, or even take a scenic ferry ride.

How to Drive on the Beach: Remember, designated areas only! Check tide tables and local regulations before you hit the gas.

How to Find a Place to Stay: From camping under the stars to cozy beach rentals, there's something for every budget and style.

How to Pack for Long Beach: Think layers, comfortable shoes for exploring, and of course, all the beach essentials: swimsuit, sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, and a beach towel (the longer, the better!).

How to Avoid Getting Lost on Such a Long Beach: Well, that might be a bit of a challenge. But hey, getting lost can lead to some pretty cool discoveries, right? Just be sure to have a charged phone and a general sense of direction (or a friend who does!).
